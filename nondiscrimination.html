Nondiscrimination

PillPack is committed to equal opportunity and nondiscrimination and prohibits discrimination on the basis of race, religion, creed, color, national origin, citizenship, marital status, sex, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran status, political ideology, ancestry, the presence of any physical, sensory, or mental disabilities, or any other legally protected status.

PillPack:

  • Provides free aids and services to people with disabilities to communicate effectively with us, such as:
    • Qualified sign language interpreters
    • Written information in other formats (large print, audio, accessible electronic formats, other formats, including prescription readers)
  • Provides free language services to people whose primary language is not English, such as:
    • Qualified interpreters
    • Information written in other languages

If you need these services, contact PillPack’s Civil Rights Coordinator.

If you believe that PillPack has failed to provide these services or discriminated in any way, you can file a grievance with: PillPack Civil Rights Coordinator, 250 Commercial St Ste 2012, Manchester NH, 1-855-745-5725, 1-603-935-9108 (fax), hello@pillpack.com. You can file a grievance in person or by mail, fax, or email. If you need help filing a grievance, the PillPack Civil Rights Coordinator is available to help you.

You can also file a civil rights complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights electronically through the Office for Civil Rights Complaint Portal, available at https://ocrportal.hhs.gov/ocr/portal/lobby.jsf, or by mail or phone at: U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, 200 Independence Avenue SW., Room 509F, HHH Building, Washington, DC 20201, 1-800-868-1019, 800-537-7697 (TDD). Complaint forms are available at http://www.hhs.gov/ocr/office/file/index.html.
